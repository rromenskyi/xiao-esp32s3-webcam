What it does

Everything a security cam should — running entirely on the board.

📹

Multi-viewer stream

MJPEG over WiFi with a responsive dark UI: resolution, quality, brightness, mirror/flip — live. Several viewers at once.

🧠

AI person detection

ESP-DL pedestrian_detect confirms a real person before recording. Curtains, light and pets stop false-triggering it.

🎬

Motion recording

Records to microSD only on motion, each event its own timestamped clip, with audio, a disk budget, and crash-safe checkpoints.

🔔

Telegram alerts

Sends the snapshot of the moment — with a caption — straight to your chat over HTTPS. Built in, no relay or server.

🎭

Ignore zones

Paint a mask over a window or a busy street so motion there is ignored. Tune the AI confidence to taste.

🕑

Timestamps done right

A per-second .srt sidecar shows the real time in any player — full quality, no lossy re-encode.

📡

Zero-touch ops

SoftAP provisioning, mDNS xiao-cam.local, multi-network roaming, NTP, and system stats.

⬆️

OTA forever

One USB flash, then update over WiFi from the web UI. Dual 3.5 MB slots.

💾

Drop-in provisioning

Back up every setting to the SD card; drop it into a fresh board and it configures itself.

How it works

Single-file ESP-IDF app. Camera in, alerts and recordings out.

              ┌──── XIAO ESP32S3 Sense ────┐
 OV2640 ─JPEG─►│ camera ─┬─► MJPEG stream (:81)     │
              │        ├─► loop recorder ► AVI+.srt ► SD
 PDM mic ────►│ mic     │    ▲                      │
              │        │  motion ► ESP-DL person? ─► record
              │ web UI/API (:80)  └─► Telegram / webhook + /event.jpg
              └───────────────────────────┐
   WiFi (roams) · mDNS xiao-cam.local · OTA-over-WiFi

Quick start

Flash once over USB, then it lives on your WiFi.

1

Flash

Build with ESP-IDF v6.0 and flash over USB once. See the build guide.

2

Provision

Join the XIAO-SETUP WiFi, pick your network, save.

3

Open

Browse to http://xiao-cam.local/. Stream is live.

4

Arm it

Turn on recording + AI + Telegram in the Recording card. Done.
